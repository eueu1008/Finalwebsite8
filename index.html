1952


Warhol’s first exhibition presents
drawings based on the writings
of Truman Capote


1956

Warhol’s homoerotic drawings
and gold shoe drawings are
exhibited in New York


1962

Warhol makes his first photo-silkscreen
pictures, and experiments with stamps
and stencils. Campbell’s Soup Cans
are shown at the Ferus Gallery, Los
Angeles
Warhol participates in The New Realists
exhibition in New York, the first important
survey of Pop art
Also has a solo exhibition at The Stable
Gallery

1977

Warhol’s folk-art collection is
exhibited at the Museum of
American Folk Art


1985

He exhibits the Collaboration Works,
produced jointly with
the young artist Jean-Michel
Basquiat

1987

Warhol visits Milan for an exhibition
of his Last Supper pictures On February 22 Warhol dies
unexpectedly in a New York hospital
following a gall bladder operation
